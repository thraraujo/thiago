Thiago Araujo

I am a theoretical physicist currently working at the Institute for Theoretical Physics in the University of Bern as a postdoc researcher.

I finished my Ph.D. at the Institute for Theoretical Physics in the Sao Paulo State University under the supervision of Horatiu Nastase. I also spent three years in South Korea as a postdoc at the Asia Pacific Center for Theoretical Physics.

Research

My research interests span the following topics: 2D integrable and conformal systems, the AdS/CFT correspondence and the construction of supergravity solutions. Representative papers are highlighted.

Towards Yang-Baxter integrability of quantum crystal melting: From Kagome lattice to vertex models
Thiago Araujo
arXiv [hep-th], 2020
arXiv

I tried to study Yang-Baxter integrability of a plane partition configurations using a map to a Kagome lattice system.

Quantum crystals, Kagome lattice and plane partitions fermion-boson duality
Thiago Araujo, Domenico Orlando, Susanne Reffert
arXiv [hep-th], 2020
arXiv / Inspire-HEP

Quantum integrability for a Hamiltonian with plane partition grading. We studied several algebraic aspects of this Hamiltonian; we tried, in particular, to study the states in terms of representations of the affine Yangian of gl(1).

Nonlocal charges from marginal deformations of 2D CFTs
Thiago Araujo
Phys.Rev.D 101, 2020
arXiv / Inspire-HEP

In this paper we study generic features of nonlocal charges obtained from marginal deformations of WZNW models.

Holographic integration of TT and JT via O(d,d)
Thiago Araujo Eoin O Colgain, Yuho Sakatani Mohammmad Mahdi Sheikh-Jabbari Hossein Yavartanoo
HEP 03 168, 2019
arXiv / Inspire-HEP

Integrable deformations of 2D conformal field theories.
